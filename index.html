what we should have in common

We both like to think out of the box to find a different perspective. We also like straightforward and simple solutions. We do not imitate others, instead we try to understand and extend.

We share our experiences with others and choose to participate in open source development and collaboration platforms. We do this not just as a gesture but instead we use this open environment to expand ourselves.

We are both eager to make things easier to understand and more secure to use. Systems can only be secure if people understand and like to use them. It's a matter of incentives not restrictions.

what i can do

I am working on small-scale web projects on the LAMPP stack, the latest one is a mobile web application. I am pretty good at administrating Debian-based Linux systems and I find my way around RedHat-based ones as well. I also know the basics of the Apache httpd and I can also do some MySQL administration.
I do most of my PHP programming with Zend Framework but I have used micro-frameworks like Limonade as well. My web applications rely on HTML5 and the Ajax libraries jQuery and jQuery Mobile.
I am organizing my projects with wikis, issue trackers and revision control systems.

I am straightforward and not afraid talking about touchy issues but I always have a very constructive and polite approach towards it. I am also very open to others and can take constructive criticism pretty well.
If there is a conflict I tend to analyze it and resolve it by exchanging perspectives, if people need guidance I can often provide that as well.

I like to get creative and although I think this is clearly not my major domain I think I got some insight into the whys and whens of creative people. I do some graphics myself sometimes but it is really just a hobby.

I follow Getting Things Done as an organizational pattern. I am not a fanboy but I learned to appreciate it a lot. I am also trying out lifehacks as I see fit. I think of it as just an extension to my overall do-it-yourself approach.
Not everything about organization is fancy and exciting though but some things just have to be done, right?

what i am looking for

There are several fields of improvement I am working on:
First of all I find everything about communication exciting, be it between people or between man and machines. Trying to figure this out helps me with people but also with ergonomics and security.
Then there is the vast domain of security. I like to see any security as a system and most of the time it is vast and incomprehensible. That is why we need more interdisciplinary work on this - everything is entangled.
And of course improving my self-organization plays a big role, too.

For the near future I am searching for a web development project that lets me combine communication, security, ergonomics and organization in a neat user interface.
